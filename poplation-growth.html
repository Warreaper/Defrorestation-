THE IMPACT OF US

    Before deforestation:


  1. Forests serve as crucial ecosystems, providing habitat for diverse plant and animal species.
  2. They act as carbon sinks, absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and regulating the climate.
  3. Forests maintain water cycles, prevent soil erosion, and provide resources like timber, food, and medicine.

      4. After deforestation:


      1. Cleared land replaces lush forests, often for agriculture, urban development, or logging.
      2. Loss of habitat leads to a decline in biodiversity. Soil erosion increases, disrupting water patterns and causing sedimentation in rivers and streams.
      3. Release of stored carbon exacerbates climate change, impacting global weather patterns and ecosystems.

          4. Overall, deforestation results in ecological imbalance, loss of ecosystem services, and negative consequences for both local communities and the global environment.
